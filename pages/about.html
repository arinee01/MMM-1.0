About the Team

The MMMM Project

Everyday Magic is a comprehensive digital companion app for museum exhibitions, created as part of the "Information Modeling and Web technologies" course at the University of Bologna. Our project explores the intersection of cultural heritage, digital technology, and user experience design.

The MMMM framework (Multimedia Museum Meta Mirror) demonstrates how digital tools can enhance physical museum experiences by providing contextual information, multiple narrative paths, and adaptive content presentation based on visitor needs and preferences.

Our project focuses on mystical traditions and protective symbols across cultures and time periods, creating an immersive digital experience that helps visitors understand the rich cultural heritage behind these artifacts.

Project Goals

  • Accessibility: Create content that adapts to different user needs and knowledge levels
  • Cultural Preservation: Digitally preserve and present cultural artifacts in meaningful ways
  • Educational Value: Provide learning opportunities for diverse audiences
  • Technical Innovation: Demonstrate modern web technologies for cultural applications
  • User Experience: Create intuitive and engaging digital museum experiences

Academic Context

This project is part of the Master's Degree in Digital Humanities and Digital Knowledge (DHDK) at the University of Bologna. The course focuses on applying digital technologies to humanities research and cultural heritage preservation.
